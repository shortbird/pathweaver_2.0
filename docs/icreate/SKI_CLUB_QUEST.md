**Status:** Ready to author · **Org:** iCreate (org-gated) · **Quest type:** `class` (credit-class) · **XP target:** 1,000

This is the complete, authoring-ready build of the iCreate Ski Club adventure quest.
It maps 1:1 onto Optio primitives: 1 Quest → 8 Lessons ("missions") → suggested Tasks.
Whoever authors it (Molly / Marika) can drop each block straight into the quest builder.

**Pillar keys:** `stem` (STEM & Logic) · `art` (Arts & Creativity) · `communication`
(Language & Communication) · `civics` (Society & Culture) · `wellness` (Life & Wellness).

**XP scale:** current halved scale, 25 = floor. ~1,625 XP is on the menu; a student reaches
the 1,000-XP credit target by choosing their own path. Ski-day logs (Mission 3) are the
repeatable PE backbone.

**Evidence** for every task = photo / video / GPS screenshot / trail map / written reflection
uploaded to the journal, which flows into the portfolio and transcript.

**Success criteria** below follow the constraint / quality / process model — each is a
checkable bar, not a vague description. They set the difficulty of the task.

## Quest-level copy

**Title:** iCreate Ski Club

**Short description (catalog card):**
Get outside, challenge yourself, and turn a season on the mountain into real learning. Ski
when it works for your family, document your adventures, and earn credit across science,
math, writing, media, and more — all at your own pace.

**Full description (quest detail page):**
The iCreate Ski Club is a self-paced adventure quest. You choose when and where you ski all
season long while completing missions that weave together physical activity, science,
engineering, math, geography, media, and reflection. There are no required meeting times —
just you, the mountain, and a growing portfolio of everything you learned. Optional community
meetups and group ski days may be announced throughout the season. Complete missions, upload
your evidence, and earn your credit one run at a time.

> **Safety & responsibility:** All skiing happens under the supervision of your parent,
> guardian, or a designated responsible adult. iCreate provides the learning framework and
> community, not transportation, on-slope supervision, instruction, or medical services. Your
> family assumes all risks of skiing and snowboarding and follows all resort rules. A club
> liability waiver is signed at sign-up, separate from the general iCreate waiver.

## Mission 1 — Base Camp

**Lesson blurb (student-facing):**
Every expedition starts with preparation. Before your first run, set your goals for the
season, learn the mountain safety code, and get your gear dialed in. This is your launch pad —
come back to your goals all season to see how far you have climbed.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 1.1 | Set your season goals | wellness | 50 |
| 1.2 | Learn the mountain safety code | wellness | 50 |
| 1.3 | Gear check | stem | 25 |
| 1.4 | Read the mountain | civics | 25 |

**1.1 Set your season goals** — Write three goals for the season: one physical, one academic,
one personal.
- Names one measurable physical goal (e.g. "ski 20,000 vertical feet," "link parallel turns").
- Names one academic goal tied to a mission you are excited about.
- Names one personal goal (courage, consistency, trying something new) in your own words.

**1.2 Learn the mountain safety code** — Learn "Your Responsibility Code" and explain what it
means for how you will ski.
- Lists at least five points of the Responsibility Code.
- Explains, in one or two sentences each, what two of those points look like in real life.
- States one thing you will personally do differently to ski responsibly.

**1.3 Gear check** — Photograph your ski setup and label what each piece does.
- Photo clearly shows skis/board, boots, bindings, poles/helmet.
- Each major piece is labeled with its name and one-sentence job.
- Notes one thing you checked for safety (binding tension, helmet fit, edge condition).

**1.4 Read the mountain** — Read a real trail map and explain the difficulty rating system.
- Includes a photo or screenshot of an actual resort trail map.
- Explains what green circle, blue square, and black diamond mean.
- Identifies one run you want to try and why it fits your current skill.

**Subtotal: 150**

## Mission 2 — First Tracks

**Lesson blurb:**
This is the one you will remember: your first day on snow this season. Ski it, feel it, and
then capture it — the nerves, the wins, the wipeouts. First Tracks is about noticing your own
experience, not perfecting it.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 2.1 | Complete your first ski day | wellness | 50 |
| 2.2 | First-day reflection | communication | 50 |
| 2.3 | Catch the moment | art | 25 |

**2.1 Complete your first ski day** — Get on the mountain and log the details.
- Records the date, resort, and snow/weather conditions.
- Includes a photo of you on the mountain that day.
- Notes how many runs you took or how long you skied.

**2.2 First-day reflection** — Write about your first day. Suggested angle: "How I overcame
fear" or "My first day back."
- At least 150 words in your own voice.
- Describes one specific moment (a run, a fall, a view, a feeling), not just a summary.
- Ends with what you want to work on next time.

**2.3 Catch the moment** — Take one strong action or scene photo from your first day.
- Photo is from your actual first day on the mountain.
- Composition shows motion, place, or emotion (not just a posed selfie).
- One caption line explains what is happening and why you chose this shot.

**Subtotal: 125**

## Mission 3 — Vertical Explorer

**Lesson blurb:**
Now you build. Vertical Explorer is your season-long engine: log your ski days, stack up
vertical feet, chase an endurance goal, and watch a new skill click into place. Log every day
you ski here — this is where the miles add up.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 3.1 | Log a ski day (repeatable) | wellness | 25 |
| 3.2 | Track your vertical | stem | 75 |
| 3.3 | Chase an endurance goal | wellness | 50 |
| 3.4 | Level up a skill | wellness | 50 |

**3.1 Log a ski day** *(log this one every time you ski)* — Record a day on the mountain and
the vertical feet you skied.
- Records date, resort, and vertical feet (from a ski-tracker app, resort stats, or estimate).
- Includes one photo or note from the day.
- Can be completed once per ski day across the season.

**3.2 Track your vertical** — Total your vertical feet across three or more days and chart your
progress.
- Uses data from at least three logged ski days.
- Presents a chart, table, or graph of vertical over time.
- Writes one or two sentences on the trend you see (improving? plateauing? why?).

**3.3 Chase an endurance goal** — Set and reach a personal endurance goal.
- States the goal before the ski day (full day, most runs yet, longest continuous run, etc.).
- Provides evidence you hit it (log, screenshot, photo).
- Reflects on how your body felt and what made it possible.

**3.4 Level up a skill** — Document a new skill you learned or improved.
- Names the specific skill (parallel turns, first blue run, riding switch, moguls).
- Shows before/after evidence — video, photo, or a clear written account.
- Explains one thing that finally made it click.

**Subtotal: 200**

## Mission 4 — Snow Scientist

**Lesson blurb:**
The mountain is a physics lab. Why does powder feel like floating? Why does one slope slide
and another hold? Pick a question that grabs you and dig into the science behind the snow under
your skis.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 4.1 | How snow forms | stem | 50 |
| 4.2 | Why powder floats | stem | 50 |
| 4.3 | Avalanche & weather | stem | 75 |
| 4.4 | Friction & motion | stem | 50 |

*(Students pick one or two — this mission is a menu, not a checklist.)*

**4.1 How snow forms** — Explain how snow forms and what makes powder different from hardpack.
- Describes how a snowflake forms in a cloud.
- Explains what makes snow light/dry (powder) versus heavy/wet or icy (hardpack).
- Connects it to something you actually felt skiing this season.

**4.2 Why powder floats** — Explain why wide powder skis float better than narrow skis.
- Explains the role of surface area and how it spreads your weight.
- Uses a comparison (wide vs. narrow, or snowshoe vs. boot) to make it click.
- Includes a diagram, sketch, or photo to support the explanation.

**4.3 Avalanche & weather** — Write a short report on avalanche science or mountain weather
systems.
- Explains at least two factors that make a slope more or less likely to slide, OR how a
  weather system moves snow onto the mountains.
- Uses at least one real source (article, avalanche center, forecast) and names it.
- 200+ words, in your own words, with one takeaway for staying safe.

**4.4 Friction & motion** — Explain the physics of why wax matters or how edges grip.
- Explains friction between ski base and snow, and how wax changes it.
- OR explains how an edged ski carves instead of skidding.
- Ties the concept to a specific moment you noticed it on snow.

**Subtotal: 225**

## Mission 5 — Mountain Engineer

**Lesson blurb:**
Someone designed everything that gets you up and down the mountain — your skis, your bindings,
the chairlift, even the snow itself on a dry day. Pick one piece of mountain engineering and
figure out how it actually works.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 5.1 | How skis are built | stem | 50 |
| 5.2 | How bindings work | stem | 50 |
| 5.3 | Lifts & snowmaking | stem | 75 |

**5.1 How skis are built** — Explain how a ski or snowboard is constructed.
- Describes at least three parts of ski construction (core, edges, base, camber/rocker).
- Explains how camber or rocker changes how a ski behaves.
- Includes a labeled diagram or cross-section sketch.

**5.2 How bindings work** — Explain how ski bindings release and why that matters.
- Explains what a binding does in a fall and why release protects you.
- Explains what a DIN setting is and what it depends on (weight, skill, boot size).
- States why the correct setting matters for safety.

**5.3 Lifts & snowmaking** — Explain how a chairlift or snowmaking system works.
- Chooses one system (chairlift OR snowmaking) and explains its main working parts.
- Explains the core principle (cable haul loop, or forcing water + air into snow).
- Includes a diagram, photo, or clear step-by-step of the process.

**Subtotal: 175**

## Mission 6 — Trail Storyteller

**Lesson blurb:**
A great ski day is also a great story. Grab your camera, your phone, or your GoPro and turn
your time on snow into something other people can feel. This mission is about seeing the
mountain like an artist.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 6.1 | Ski photo essay | art | 75 |
| 6.2 | Highlight reel | art | 100 |
| 6.3 | Action photography | art | 50 |

**6.1 Ski photo essay** — Build a photo essay that tells the story of a day or the season.
- At least five photos arranged in an intentional order.
- Each photo has a caption that moves the story forward.
- The set has a clear arc (a day, a progression, a place) — not random shots.

**6.2 Highlight reel** — Edit your GoPro or phone footage into a highlight reel.
- Final video is 30 seconds to 3 minutes.
- Includes at least three different clips or angles, cut to a rhythm or music.
- Has a title and a clear beginning and end.

**6.3 Action photography** — Shoot a set of action photographs on the mountain.
- At least three photos capturing motion (a turn, a jump, spray, speed).
- Demonstrates one photography choice on purpose (panning, framing, timing, angle).
- One sentence per photo on what you were going for.

**Subtotal: 225**

## Mission 7 — Resort Reviewer

**Lesson blurb:**
You are becoming a mountain expert — so start thinking like one. Compare resorts, follow the
water downhill, and run the numbers on what a season on snow really costs. This mission turns
your experience into research others can use.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 7.1 | Resort head-to-head | civics | 75 |
| 7.2 | Follow the water | civics | 50 |
| 7.3 | Pass vs. tickets | stem | 50 |
| 7.4 | Rent vs. own | civics | 50 |

**7.1 Resort head-to-head** — Compare two Utah ski resorts.
- Compares two resorts across at least three factors (terrain, amenities, price, vibe, access).
- Uses specifics (named runs, real prices, actual features), not just opinions.
- Ends with a recommendation for a specific kind of skier and why.

**7.2 Follow the water** — Study the watershed your resort sits in.
- Identifies the watershed or canyon and where the snowmelt eventually goes.
- Includes a map showing the path from mountain to reservoir/river/lake.
- Explains one reason the watershed matters to people downstream.

**7.3 Pass vs. tickets** — Run a cost analysis of a season pass versus day tickets.
- Uses real prices for a pass and for single-day tickets.
- Calculates the break-even number of ski days.
- States which option fits your season and shows the math.

**7.4 Rent vs. own** — Break down the cost of renting versus owning gear.
- Compares a season of rentals against buying (new or used) for one gear item.
- Includes real prices and a clear total for each option.
- Gives a recommendation for a first-year skier and explains the trade-off.

**Subtotal: 225**

## Mission 8 — Summit Challenge

**Lesson blurb:**
This is the top of the mountain. Pull your whole season together, lead someone else into the
sport, and present what you learned through your portfolio. Summit Challenge is where a season
of runs becomes a story of growth.

| # | Task | Pillar | XP |
|---|------|--------|----|
| 8.1 | Season showcase | communication | 100 |
| 8.2 | Bring a friend | civics | 75 |
| 8.3 | Organize a ski day | civics | 75 |
| 8.4 | Advice for beginners | communication | 50 |

**8.1 Season showcase** — Complete your season-long goal and present it through your portfolio.
- Revisits the goals you set in Base Camp and reports how each turned out.
- Pulls together evidence from at least three missions into one presentation (doc, slides, or
  video).
- Reflects on the single biggest thing you learned or overcame this season.

**8.2 Bring a friend** — Introduce a friend or sibling to skiing and document it.
- Describes who you brought and what you taught them.
- Includes a photo or short video from the day.
- Reflects on what it was like to be the teacher instead of the learner.

**8.3 Organize a ski day** — Plan and lead a family or club ski day.
- Produces a real plan: resort, date, who is coming, timing, and what to bring.
- Includes a route or run plan for the group by ability.
- Reflects afterward on what went well and what you would change.

**8.4 Advice for beginners** — Write an "Advice for Beginners" guide.
- At least five concrete, useful tips a first-timer could actually follow.
- Covers more than one area (gear, safety, technique, mindset).
- Written in a friendly, encouraging voice.

**Subtotal: 300**

## Authoring & ops notes

- **Repeatable task:** 3.1 (Log a ski day) is the only task meant to be logged many times. If
  the builder has no formal repeat flag, students use the create-your-own-task flow to log each
  additional day; everything else is once.
- **Lesson content fields:** use the blurb above verbatim, or trim to 2 sentences. Keep it
  light — the doing is the learning (just-in-time teaching).
- **Credit vs. XP:** authored here as a `class` credit-class (completion via
  `quests.class_review_status='credit_awarded'`, 1,000-XP target). If iCreate wants a
  non-credit version first, author the identical quest with a normal (non-`class`) type — all
  content is reusable.
- **Community group:** create the roster-synced class messaging group for "Ski Club" so
  announcements, meetups, and photo-sharing have a home. Announcement-only posts cover the
  optional group ski days.
- **Registration:** add the $500 season SKU to the iCreate funnel and attach the club liability
  waiver as an e-sign document (separate from the general iCreate waiver).
- **Season pass stays off-platform:** arrange a resort group discount code; families buy passes
  directly from the resort. Do not sell or distribute passes through iCreate.
