# PART 1 — Why Optio Is Different

## Slide 1 — Title
**Optio: Learning That Students Actually Want to Do**
Subhead: An accredited, student-directed model that turns curriculum into real, documented learning.

## Slide 2 — The Core Idea
**"The Process Is the Goal."**
Optio celebrates learning as it happens — not seat time, not completion clicks, not grades.
- Students do real work, document it, and earn credit for demonstrated learning.
- The diploma is the byproduct of a meaningful journey, not the point of it.

---

## Slide 3 — The Reality of Online School Today (the contrast)
How students actually "do" traditional online courses:
- Let the videos play on mute.
- Use AI to answer the multiple-choice questions.
- Earn the credit. Learn nothing.

**Optio flips it:** there's no video to mute and no multiple-choice to game. The student *has* to do real work and show evidence of it — and they actually want to.

---

## Slide 4 — Utah Already Has a Name for This (the policy anchor)
Optio's model isn't experimental — the **Utah State Board of Education already created the category for it.**
USBE Administrative Rule **R277-419 (Pupil Accounting)** recognizes two kinds of programs:
- **Attendance Validated** — students counted (and funded) by *physically attending* a brick-and-mortar school. Seat time.
- **Learner Validated** — students counted (and funded) by *demonstrated learning* through online, blended, or **personalized, competency-based** programs.

**Optio is a learner-validated model — exactly the category the State Board defined and funds.**
Utah has incentivized this since 2016 (Competency-Based Education grants; Title 53F-5, Part 5).

---

## Slide 5 — What Makes Optio Unique (the benefits grid)
Six differentiators — each praised by students and parents *unprompted*:
1. **Work in any order** — non-linear, student-directed. No forced lockstep.
2. **Not graded** — mastery and growth, not points and ranking.
3. **Flexible evidence** — type it, photograph handwritten notes, use the digital notebook, link a project, record a video.
4. **AI step generator** — students break big tasks into doable steps with AI scaffolding.
5. **Recognize learning anywhere** — a dinner-table conversation or a game with friends can become documented learning.
6. **Transparent progress** — students see an accurate, real-time read on where they are.

## Slide 7 — Why It Works (the learning science — and my research)
Optio is built on Self-Determination Theory — the three drivers of intrinsic motivation:
- **Choice** — students choose their paths, evidence, and pacing.
- **Connection** — reflection and real-world application make learning personal.
- **Competence** — tasks meet students at the right challenge level, with AI to scaffold.

**This isn't just theory — it's my doctoral research.** My dissertation studied the impact of **autonomous learning strategies on student intrinsic motivation** — conducted with students right here at **American Fork High School**. Optio is that research, built into a platform.

---

## Slide 8 — Built for How Students Live (the mobile app)
Optio's mobile app makes learning part of everyday life:
- **Capture learning moments** — document learning the instant it happens, from anywhere.
- **Parent dashboard** — parents see real progress and stay involved.
- **Supporters who cheer them on** — extended family and friends can follow a student's learning journey and encourage them, like a private, positive social feed built around real growth.
- **Bounties** — fresh, optional challenges that invite students to go further.

## Utah Policy & Funding Backing (verified — for Slides 4 & 14 + Q&A)

The State Board already created the legal category Optio fits — and funds districts to build it.

**USBE Administrative Rule R277-419 (Pupil Accounting)** — amended March 19, 2020, creating two program categories for membership/WPU funding:
- **Attendance Validated Program** — R277-419-2(3): *"a program within an LEA that consists of eligible, enrolled public school students who physically attend school in a brick and mortar school."*
- **Learner Validated Program** — R277-419-2(16): *"a program within an LEA that consists of eligible, enrolled public school students where the student receives instruction through: (a) an online learning program; (b) a blended learning program; or (c) a personalized, competency-based learning program."*

**Learner-validated funding requirements (R277-419-5):**
- LEA adopts a **written policy** designating a "learner validated enrollment measurement" to document each student's membership status.
- LEA must **engage with the student at least once every ten consecutive school days** (LMS login or instructor contact).
- Optional components: minimum login/teacher-contact requirements, hourly engagement minimums, assignment-completion timelines.

**PCBL Grant Program (the funding angle):**
- **Personalized, Competency-Based Learning (PCBL) Grants** support LEAs to **plan, implement, scale, and expand** competency-based education.
- Structure: a **Planning grant (~5 yrs of funding if expectations met)**, with the chance to apply for **additional years** of implementation + expansion.
- **Eligibility:** open to LEAs (districts and charters). **Prerequisite:** at least **two LEA representatives** (incl. the superintendent or charter director) must complete the PCBL grant **technical-assistance training** before applying.
- Apply by submitting an application to the State Superintendent; a review committee recommends Board approval.
- **USBE contacts:** Nathan Auck (nathan.auck@schools.utah.gov), Todd Call (todd.call@schools.utah.gov).
- **Honest framing:** the grant funds the *LEA's* competency-based initiative; whether Optio's specific costs are covered depends on the district's approved plan. Optio's role = the implementation platform + application support. Don't promise the grant "pays for Optio" outright.

**Statutory + grant backbone:**
- **Utah Code Title 53F, Chapter 5, Part 5 — Personalized, Competency-based Learning Grants Program** (key section **53F-5-502**).
- **USBE Rule R277-712** implements the grants.
- Utah has funded/incentivized competency-based education via the **Competency-Education Grants Program since 2016**.

**The one-line takeaway for the room:** *Optio is a learner-validated program. Utah doesn't just permit this model — the State Board defined it, regulates it, and funds it.*
