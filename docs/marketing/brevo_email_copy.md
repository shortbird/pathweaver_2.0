# Brevo Email Copy — Free Class Nurture + Segment Templates

Voice: friendly, positive, concise, helpful. Not overly excited, not wordy. Plain and personal,
signed **Dr. Bowman**, sent from tanner@optioeducation.com with a live reply-to. No emojis.
**No em dashes.** "Class" language, not "credit" (credit only where mechanically necessary).

Positioning rules:
- **App-first.** Primary CTA is downloading the iOS/Android app (official store badge buttons,
  hosted in the Brevo gallery). Web gets a brief mention, never the headline.
- **Students create their class.** The flow is: choose a name, choose the subject you want to earn
  credit for, then personalize assignments (called tasks) by adding your own or using AI
  suggestions. Never "browse" or "pick from a catalog."
- **$149 per class** after the free one.
- **Never mention the possibility of a school not accepting the transfer.** No refund framing.
- Free-class sequence can talk transcripts and accreditation. **K-12 family emails (Families, POE,
  newsletter) lead with features instead**: portfolio, supporters, quests, custom classes, the app.

The full details are in [Transcripts and Accreditation](/docs/high-school-classes/transcripts-and-accreditation).

### Email 4 — day 7
**Subject:** What an Optio class actually looks like

No lectures. No worksheets. Here's the actual shape of it:

You create your class and fill it with real projects, done at your own pace. As you go, you capture evidence right from your phone: photos, videos, writing, the work itself. It all builds into a portfolio you can show people. An Optio teacher reviews everything before the class counts. Here is [what teachers look for](/docs/high-school-classes/teacher-review-and-earning-credit).

You can also invite supporters. Parents, grandparents, and mentors can see your progress and cheer you on.

The point isn't to race through it. It's to spend your time on work you're proud of, and have it count.
