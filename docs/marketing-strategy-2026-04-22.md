# Optio Online Marketing Strategy

**Date:** April 22, 2026
**Goal of meeting:** Figure out a narrow launch approach that gets students into their first Optio class for free, and let retention do the heavy lifting after that.

---

## 1. Core Marketing Position

**Conversion line:** *Turn your hobbies into a high school class.*
**Brand line:** *Real credit for real life.*

The conversion line goes on ads, the landing page hero, email subject lines — anywhere a cold student needs to understand what Optio is in one beat. "Class" is deliberate: kids count their schedule in classes, not credits.

The brand line does memory work, not explanation. It lives on the homepage header, Instagram bio, email footer, merch — places where someone already knows Optio and we want a phrase that sticks.

Everything we put in front of a student is supported by the same two beats:
- **Offer:** First class free.
- **Trust:** Transfer Guarantee.

---

## 2. Core Offer

| Product | Launch offer | List price | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Individual class (transfer credit) | First class free | $150 / class (one semester, 0.5 credit) | Top-of-funnel. We eat the CAC on the product side instead of the ad-spend side. |
| Optio Academy (full-time) | No discount | $8,000 / year (Utah Fits All and similar ESAs may cover the full cost) | Different audience, not the focus of this launch. |

The bet behind "first class free" is simple: once a student actually finishes an Optio class, they'll want to do another. If that holds, free removes the only real objection in the way of volume, and the Transfer Guarantee stops being a promise and turns into a demonstration. The whole thing breaks if first-class completers don't enroll in class #2, which is why that's the one number we watch closest.

Two things a student always wants to know:

1. What does it cost me? **First class free.**
2. Will my school accept it? **Transfer Guarantee — full refund if they don't.**

Every ad and every email anchors on those two.

## 3. The Creative Template – "Your Hobby Is a High School Class"

| Activity they already do | Credit it becomes |
|---|---|
| Piano / private music lessons | Music credit |
| Club / rec sports season | PE credit |
| Summer camp | Science credit |
| Art classes, personal art practice | Fine arts credit |
| Coding projects, personal builds | Technology credit |
| Volunteer work, community involvement | Civics credit |

Each ad is one activity, one credit, free first class. No "we're different" pitches, no manifesto posts. Six activity verticals give us six creative lanes we can test independently and scale whichever ones win.

---

## 4. Target Audience

For the whole launch we're going after U.S. high schoolers, 13–18. They own the feeds we're buying on. They have the activity and the identity around it. Because the first class is free, we don't need a parent's credit card to convert — the student can self-enroll with parental consent (already a standard part of signup) and try the product on their own terms.

Targeting leans into student identity around the activity itself, not "high schooler" in the abstract. Piano kids, club soccer players, teen coders, summer-camp alumni. Every row in the table above is its own lookalike seed.

---

## 5. Channel Plan – Phased

Launch is deliberately narrow: one message, one audience, two surfaces. Channels get added as we learn what's working, not all at once.

### Phase 1: student-facing paid only

1. **TikTok.** Paid plus organic. Creative template is student doing the activity, on-screen text saying "Your [piano / soccer / camp] is a high school class. First class free," a ten-second explainer, then the CTA.
2. **Instagram Reels.** Same creative, recut for IG. Runs through Meta Ads Manager, same pipes as Facebook, so no extra ops overhead.
3. **YouTube Shorts.** Publish same ads here.

That's the whole launch. Three surfaces, one message, one metric that matters: cost per first-class enrollment. We want enough signups in 60 days to get a real read on first-class → second-class retention.

### Phase 2: added once launch data is in

4. **Parent-facing Meta.** Facebook and Instagram paid, parents 35–55. Different creative from the student side: "free up a period," "accredited transfer credit," "Utah Fits All eligible." Layered in after we've got 30 days of student-side CAC to benchmark against.
5. **Google search.** Low volume, high intent. "high school credit for [X]," "PE credit alternative," "transfer credits to high school," "accredited online classes high school."
6. **Reddit.** r/homeschool, r/teenagers, r/ApplyingToCollege, and activity-specific subs. Organic first. Promoted posts only if the organic read looks promising.

### Phase 3: once unit economics are proven

7. **Creator partnerships.** One creator per activity vertical, eventually. Skipping at launch because it's ops-heavy — outreach, contracts, affiliate tracking — and we'd be paying creators to test creative we haven't even validated ourselves yet. Better to do this after launch, when we know what converts and can hand that creative to creators who can voice it in their niche.
8. **YouTube pre-roll** on activity-adjacent content (how-to-piano, coding tutorials, soccer drills).
9. **Podcast host reads** on homeschool and parenting podcasts.
